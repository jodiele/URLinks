Website Safety

Common Website Threats

Phishing, Malware Distribution, and Deceptive Content are forms of online threats with the goal of gathering sensitive information, and have the common factor of utilizing websites for such.

Phishing:

  • Most common type of social engineering attack
  • Typically takes the form of an email impersonating a legitimate source with links to malicious websites or malware images
  • Other types include Smishing (SMS) and Vishing (voice calls)

Malware Distribution

  • Software designed to exploit devices, services, or networks
  • Distributed through malicious links, email attachments, or even legitimate websites
  • Used to steal data, disrupt services, or gain unauthorized access

Deceptive Content

  • Misleads users into giving up personal info or downloading malware
  • Includes fake websites, pop-ups, and misleading social posts
  • Often combined with phishing for higher success

Tips to Ensure Your Website Safety:

  • HTTPS: Ensure data encryption between the site and users
  • Padlock icon: Indicates a valid SSL certificate and secure connection
  • URL length & typos: Watch for shortened or misspelled URLs

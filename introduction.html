Introduction to Cybersecurity and Online Safety

Cybersecurity: Unmasking Social Engineering

What is Social Engineering in Cybersecurity?

Social engineering is referred to as a tactic of manipulation, deceit, and influence towards a victim to gain control over a computer system. This is widely utilized to steal personal and/or financial information. One of the most common ways for social engineering to thrive is within malicious links, which can be distributed in multiple ways.

What Makes Social Engineering So Dangerous for Website Safety?

Though social engineering attacks are frequent, not everyone needs to be targeted. If one successfully compromises sensitive information through these tactics, they can divulge enough details to trigger a domino effect of massive attacks with severe damage to organizations. With how sophisticated these attacks have grown, users must be more vigilant in determining the safety of their online interactions.

Examples of Social Engineering on Websites:

  • Phishing
  • Malware Distribution
